About Porteau Cove

Porteau Cove Provincial Park is the last stretch of shoreline before the highway climbs toward Squamish - an oceanfront park famous for its pier, its scuba diving, and its sunsets. It's also home to one of the only public boat launches on the east side of Howe Sound, which is exactly where we'll meet you.

What You'll See

From the cove we run straight into the heart of the fjord: granite sea walls, waterfalls dropping into the ocean, and the islands of the lower Sound. Harbour seals, bald eagles, sea lions, and herons are the regulars, with dolphins and whales passing through as Howe Sound's marine recovery continues.

Perfect For

Campers staying at the provincial park, Vancouverites who want a Howe Sound tour without the full Sea to Sky drive, photographers chasing golden hour, and anyone marking a special occasion - there are worse places to propose than a sunset on the fjord.

Getting There

Porteau Cove is 45 minutes north of downtown Vancouver and 20 minutes south of Squamish on Highway 99. Free day-use parking is available at the park. We'll confirm the exact meeting spot at the boat launch when you book.

Porteau Cove Tour FAQ

Are there boat tours from Porteau Cove?

Yes - we depart directly from the Porteau Cove boat launch, so there's no need to drive to Squamish. Sunrise and sunset wildlife tours are our specialty from this location.

How much does it cost?

The 2-3 hour tour is $375 for up to 3 guests. The 4-5 hour Half-Day Explorer is $625 for up to 3 guests, with additional guests welcome for an added fee. Full-day and custom trips are quoted per charter - call (604) 849-8898.

When should we book a sunset tour?

Sunset times on Howe Sound range from about 4:30pm in December to 9:30pm in June. We'll time your departure so you're in the best spot on the water for golden hour - just tell us your date and we'll handle the rest.

Can we combine a tour with camping at Porteau Cove?

Absolutely - it's the perfect combo. Book a sunset tour for your first evening or a sunrise tour before you pack up. The boat launch is a short walk from the campground.
