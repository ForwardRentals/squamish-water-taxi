The Best Boat Tours, Rides & Rentals in Squamish and Howe Sound for 2026

February 11, 2026 | Squamish Water Taxi

Squamish, British Columbia sits at the northern tip of Howe Sound -- one of the most spectacular fjords on Canada's west coast. While most visitors know Squamish for rock climbing at the Stawamus Chief or mountain biking in the Sea to Sky corridor, the real hidden gem is getting out on the water. Whether you're looking for a scenic boat ride, a guided boat tour, or a boat rental in Squamish, there's no better way to experience the stunning coastal mountains, islands, and wildlife of Howe Sound.

Why Squamish Is the Best Place for Boat Tours in BC

Located just 45 minutes north of Vancouver along the Sea to Sky Highway, Squamish is the gateway to Howe Sound -- a fjord stretching over 40 kilometres from Horseshoe Bay to Squamish. This dramatic waterway is flanked by towering mountains, dotted with islands like Anvil Island and Gambier Island, and home to an incredible diversity of marine wildlife.

What makes boat tours in Squamish so special:

  • Dramatic scenery -- Snow-capped mountains rise directly from the water, creating a landscape unlike anything else near Vancouver
  • Abundant wildlife -- Bald eagles, harbour seals, sea lions, porpoises, and even occasional orca sightings
  • Sheltered waters -- Howe Sound's fjord geography provides calmer conditions than the open ocean
  • Accessible from Vancouver -- An easy day trip from Metro Vancouver, Whistler, or anywhere along the Sea to Sky corridor
  • Multiple destinations -- Britannia Beach, Lions Bay, Porto Cove, Anvil Island, and dozens of hidden coves and beaches

Scenic Boat Rides on Howe Sound

A boat ride on Howe Sound is one of the most breathtaking experiences you can have in British Columbia. From the moment you leave the Squamish harbour, you're surrounded by coastal mountains that tower thousands of metres above the water. The views of the Tantalus Range, Mount Garibaldi, and the Stawamus Chief from the water are absolutely stunning.

Our Squamish boat rides take you through the heart of Howe Sound, past iconic landmarks including:

  • The Stawamus Chief -- See BC's most famous granite monolith from a perspective most people never get
  • Shannon Falls -- British Columbia's third-tallest waterfall, visible from the water
  • Britannia Beach -- A historic mining town with the BC Museum of Mining
  • Lions Bay -- A charming waterfront village nestled beneath The Lions peaks
  • Anvil Island -- The largest island in Howe Sound, with pristine beaches and old-growth forest

Whether you're visiting Squamish for the day or staying longer, a scenic boat ride is the perfect way to see the area from a completely different angle. It's also a fantastic option for couples, families, and groups looking for a unique thing to do in Squamish.

Wildlife Viewing Boat Tours

Howe Sound is teeming with marine life, making it one of the best spots in the Lower Mainland for wildlife boat tours. Our guided tours take you to the best viewing areas where you can spot:

  • Bald eagles -- Squamish is known as the Eagle Capital of Canada, and seeing them from the water is unforgettable
  • Harbour seals -- Often spotted basking on rocky outcrops throughout Howe Sound
  • Sea lions -- Steller and California sea lions visit Howe Sound, especially in winter and spring
  • Pacific white-sided dolphins -- Pods are regularly spotted in the deeper channels
  • Great blue herons -- Nesting colonies along the shoreline

Our captain has years of local knowledge and knows exactly where to find wildlife depending on the season and conditions. A wildlife boat tour from Squamish is a must-do experience for nature lovers and photographers alike.

Boat Rentals in Squamish

If you prefer to explore on your own, renting a boat in Squamish is the way to go. Squamish Water Taxi offers bareboat rentals of our 18-foot inflatable RIB (Rigid Inflatable Boat) equipped with a reliable 50 HP outboard engine.

Our Squamish boat rental is perfect for:

  • Self-guided exploration -- Chart your own course through Howe Sound's islands, coves, and beaches
  • Fishing trips -- Access the best fishing spots in Howe Sound at your own pace
  • Photography expeditions -- Get to remote locations and spend as much time as you need for the perfect shot

A valid boating licence (Pleasure Craft Operator Card) is required. All rentals include safety equipment such as life jackets, a first aid kit, and navigation aids. We provide a full safety briefing and local area orientation before you head out.

Fishing Charters on Howe Sound

Howe Sound offers excellent fishing opportunities, and a fishing charter from Squamish is the easiest way to get on the fish. The waters around Squamish are home to several species of Pacific salmon, lingcod, rockfish, and Dungeness crab.

Popular fishing experiences near Squamish include:

  • Salmon fishing -- Chinook, coho, pink, and chum salmon run through Howe Sound at various times from spring through fall
  • Bottom fishing -- Lingcod and rockfish on the rocky reefs and drop-offs
  • Crabbing -- Dungeness crab in the shallower bays and estuaries
  • Prawning -- Spot prawn season (May) brings some of the tastiest seafood in BC

Whether you're an experienced angler or trying fishing for the first time, our Squamish fishing charters can be tailored to your skill level and interests. We know the local waters inside and out.

Echo Falls Trailhead Shuttle

One of our most popular services is the Echo Falls boat shuttle. Echo Falls is a spectacular waterfall and hiking destination accessible via a 6 km trail with 950 metres of elevation gain. Our boat shuttle drops you off at the trailhead, saving you a long and difficult approach.

The Echo Falls shuttle is priced at $185 round trip for up to 3 passengers, with an additional $25 per extra person (maximum 6 passengers). It's the easiest way to access this hidden gem in the Squamish backcountry.

Planning Your Squamish Boat Trip

Ready to get out on the water in Squamish? Here are a few tips to help you plan:

Best Time to Visit

The peak season for boat tours in Squamish runs from June through September, when the weather is warmest and the days are longest. However, spring and fall offer their own advantages -- fewer crowds, excellent wildlife viewing, and dramatic moody skies.

What to Bring

  • Warm layers -- it's always cooler on the water, even in summer
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Camera or binoculars for wildlife spotting
  • Snacks and water
  • Motion sickness medication if you're sensitive
