Can You Rent a Boat Without a License in Squamish?

February 12, 2026 | Squamish Water Taxi

Planning a day out on Howe Sound and wondering if you can rent a boat without a license in Squamish? It is one of the most common questions we get asked. The short answer is: if you want to operate a motorized boat in Canada, you do need a licence. But there are also plenty of ways to enjoy the water without one. Here is everything you need to know about boating license requirements in BC and your options in Squamish.

Canada Requires a Pleasure Craft Operator Card

Under Canadian federal law, every person who operates a motorized pleasure craft -- whether it is a small fishing boat, a pontoon, or a RIB rental like ours -- must carry a valid Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC). This is often referred to as a boating license in BC, though technically it is a competency card rather than a traditional licence.

The PCOC requirement applies regardless of the size of the engine or the type of boat. It does not matter whether you own the vessel or are renting it for the afternoon. If you are at the helm of a motorized boat anywhere in Canadian waters, including Howe Sound, you need your card on board.

This requirement is enforced by Transport Canada and applies across all provinces and territories. Fines for operating without a valid PCOC can reach up to $250, so it is not something to overlook.

How to Get Your Pleasure Craft Operator Card

The good news is that getting your PCOC is straightforward and can be done entirely online. Here is how it works:

  • Choose an accredited course -- Several Transport Canada-approved providers offer online boating safety courses, including BOATsmart! and the Canadian Safe Boating Council
  • Complete the training -- The course covers navigation rules, right-of-way, buoys and markers, safety equipment requirements, and emergency procedures. Most people finish in 3 to 6 hours
  • Pass the exam -- You need a score of 75% or higher on the final test
  • Receive your card -- Once you pass, you can usually print a temporary card immediately. The official card arrives by mail within a few weeks
  • Cost -- Expect to pay around $50 for the course and exam

Your Pleasure Craft Operator Card is valid for life and never needs to be renewed. If you are planning a boat rental in Squamish or anywhere else in Canada, it is well worth getting your card ahead of time so you are ready to go when the weather is perfect.

What About Visitors from Other Countries?

If you are visiting British Columbia from another country, you are not required to hold a Canadian PCOC -- but you do need to carry proof of competency from your home country. This could be a boating licence, a certificate of completion from a boating safety course, or an equivalent document recognized by your national maritime authority.

International visitors can operate a motorized pleasure craft in Canada for up to 45 consecutive days with their foreign proof of competency. If you plan to stay longer than 45 days, you will need to obtain a Canadian PCOC.

If you are visiting from the United States, your state-issued boating education card or certificate is generally accepted. Visitors from other countries should check with their local boating authority to confirm what documentation qualifies before arriving in Squamish.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boating license to rent a boat in Squamish?

Yes. Anyone operating a motorized boat in Canada must carry a valid Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC). This applies to all motorized boat rentals in Squamish and across British Columbia. You do not need a license for non-motorized watercraft like canoes, kayaks, and paddleboards.

How do I get a Pleasure Craft Operator Card in BC?

You can complete a Transport Canada-accredited boating safety course online. The course typically takes 3 to 6 hours, costs around $50, and covers navigation rules, safety equipment, and emergency procedures. Once you pass the exam with a score of 75% or higher, your card is valid for life.

Can tourists from other countries rent a boat in Squamish?

International visitors can operate a motorized boat in Canada for up to 45 consecutive days as long as they carry proof of competency from their home country, such as a boating licence or safety certificate. After 45 days, a Canadian PCOC is required.

What can I do on the water in Squamish without a boating license?

You have several great options. Book a water taxi ride or guided boat tour with a licensed captain, or rent non-motorized watercraft like canoes, kayaks, and paddleboards from Squamish Canoe Rental. You can also take our Echo Falls shuttle for a scenic ride to a stunning backcountry waterfall.
